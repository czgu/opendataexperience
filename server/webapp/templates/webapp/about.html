About Nutri-Tools
﻿This site was created on February 21st, 2015. Nutri-Tools is a website that makes it easy to document what you eat. That way, you are aware of what you consume on a daily basis. Our main application called Nutrition Tracker allows you to search for the nutrition of various types of food from raw foods to mixed dishes! You may even keep track of the foods you eat so you know how much nutrients you had for any period of time.﻿

Our Project

Nutri-Tools uses a number of datasets provided by the Canadian federal government. These datasets lists the nutrients of foods for different categories. The datasets were used to meet the requirements for submitting a web application for the Canadian Open Data Experience (CODE) appathon. We took an interest into analyzing the intake of food and how it affects the quality of life. This is what drove us to create Nutri-Tools and the Nutrition Tracker application.

Our Team

Nutri-Tools is created by a group of University of Waterloo students interested in being involved in the Canadian Open Data Experience appathon while on coop. The team consists of Charlie Gu, Jack Lu, and Tim Huang. At the beginning of this project, Charlie worked in San Francisco, and Jack and Tim worked in Ottawa. Jack and Tim both worked for the Government of Canada in the Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada department. Thus, the team has strong understanding of the impact of food in our lives.
